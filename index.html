Frequently Asked Questions

Through research, education and innovation, EVCG is the driving force transforming the promise of an electric future into a reality.

What is an EVDC land lease and why is it beneficial?

An EVDC land lease is a agreement between a property owner and an EVDC operator that allows the operator to install electric vehicle charging infrastructure on the property owner's land. This can be beneficial for both parties because it allows the property owner to generate revenue from their land and provides EVDC operators with a location to install charging stations, making it more convenient for electric vehicle owners to recharge their vehicles.

How can my business get involved in this great opportunity to secure the future?

You can play your part in securing an electric future in a couple ways. First, by participating in the EVDC land lease program, you can elect to lease your land for charging station installation in exchange for a percentage of the net profits. Second, you can purchase charging infrastructure yourself and become a charging station operator.

What are the benefits of having an EVDC charger on my property?

There are many potential benefits of having an EVDC charger on your property, including generating revenue from the land lease, providing a convenient charging option for electric vehicle owners, and potentially increasing the value of your property. Additionally, having an EVDC charger can demonstrate your commitment to sustainability and support for electric vehicles.

Is my business a good fit for a charger/supercharger?

There are a few factors to consider when determining whether your business would be a good fit for a Supercharging station. Some of these factors include the location and accessibility of your business, the amount of traffic and foot traffic in the area, and the availability of parking for EVs, and your existing power set-up.

What type of electrical connection do I need for the charger?

Level 2 EV chargers typically require a 240-volt electrical connection, which is the same type of connection used for large appliances like electric stoves and clothes dryers. This type of connection can typically be installed by a licensed electrician and may require a dedicated circuit to ensure that the charger has sufficient power.

The electrical connection range required for Level 3-5 DC fast chargers (DCFCs) will vary depending on the specific charger and the EVs it is designed to charge. In general, however, these chargers require a high-power electrical connection with a voltage of at least 480 volts and an amperage of at least 125 amps. Additionally, these chargers may require specialized electrical equipment and installation by a licensed electrician.
